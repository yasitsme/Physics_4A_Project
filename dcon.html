DReCon

Data-Driven Responsive Control of Physics-Based Characters

There are millions of games in the virtual marketplace, yet only a handful are worthy of being top-rated and gain massive popularity while the rest are standard and not as appealing. So, what makes a game stand out and unique? The secret sauce to a game’s success is the utilization and integration of physics-based animations. The gameplay is the most important factor to a game’s ability to gain a user-base. In order to achieve this goal, animators work on synthesizing the interactions between characters and the environment through data-driven animations and physics principles. There have been 2 main factors: First, the use of kinematics to mimic movement, velocity, heading direction, speed, and motion style; Second, artificial intelligence used to train the character in an enclosed environment. DreCon was born and developed as a mesh of these 2 vital components. DreCon, aka. Data-Driven responsive Control of Physics-Based Characters is a character controlled using deep reinforced learning. In layman terms, DreCon is basically a human simulated environment where the characters are developed using physics in order to achieve high level responses at low runtime costs. The idea of motion matching was derived from this technology being the ability of controlling the motion of physically simulated characters through the use of deep reinforcement learning and data-driven animation techniques. The characters are expected to have the ability to move and balance using their own strength, mirroring realistic interactions within an environment. The purpose behind DreCon is to maintain a high level of responsiveness from user input while also keeping the simulated movement of the characters as natural looking as possible. Animators achieve this level following the “12 Principles of Animation” and through further research as the years progress. Through the magic of animations, animators have used illusions by playing back animations through Drecon technology in gameplay. At first, this could look very deceiving as the movement of the character would be inconsistent with physical laws of nature. These physical inconsistencies could manifest as foot sliding, floating, unnatural acceleration, unbalanced poses, limbs clipping geometry, and strange collisions. In order to achieve the optimized gameplay, a set of unique constraints and coefficients have been set to ensure low runtime and better user experience.
